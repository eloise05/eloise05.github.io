H2K


User Experience Design


Summary

H2K, is a E-Learning startup that designs an app to teach Korean to children with dyslexia. I worked as a UX Design intern, which was recruited as the first non-software developer. Nontheless, as I have front-end development skills, I was also engaged in many different projects closesly communicating with the whole company.

I designed a user flow of the reward system from end to end for a powerful user engagement. I aimed to design a reward system which would motivate users to study by giving them entertaining features.
Through interviewing multiple users, I realized the behavior of ‘taking a picture’ was regarded as enjoyable by kids.
Therefore, I designed a reward system that lets the user take pictures with specially designed filters when they finish certain chapters of the curriculum.

Design Challenge: Design a Reward System for the Sojung Hangul, the main product of H2K


Reward for Children

Unlike other apps, this app had a specific and unique user group who are children aged from 4 to 8. I had to redefine what is reward, which could give joy to the children that is enough for them to come back to the app everyday spending 10 mins.

Through observing children's behaviors and having conversations with them, I could figure out that they like popping animations with fun sound effects and visual effects that could captivate their senses. Also, they loved taking pictures! From their parents' smartphone gallery apps, I could find happy smiles on every single children's face! :)

Reward System

Finally, we designed two main reward systems.

1) Fun Filters
2) Apple Effect

Fun filter would be given to the user after finishing 3 days of lesson. Fun filters are actually connected to each other! Every filter has its story (Korean Traditional fairy tales) and it could be seen a new story as a whole.
The apple effect will be shown to the user(kid) after he/she finishes his/her daily lesson.

Learning & Takeaways

As a UX Designer, it was amazing to interact with the users and design new features of the app.
I could figure out the reward system was educationally appropriate and enjoyable for users through usability tests.
Through interviewing the users, I learned how to communicate with the users of my own words and photos.
From this experience, I discovered that I love designing creative things that could help the users, especially the kids.
