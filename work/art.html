UC Berkeley-SKKU Student Collaborative Design Workshop


Design Workshop


Summary

During the winter of 2019, I had the opportunity to participate in the Design Workshop with Bravo Lab at UC Berkeley, California. I am grateful to have had the opportunity to represent Sungkyunkwan University and play an active role in the workshop.

It was a fantastic experience that reinforced my passion for User Experience and Design Thinking!

Design Challenge
Design a meaningful mobility for college students in UC Berkeley

When the team began to engage in the workshop, we had conversations to be on the same page of what we understand about 'meaningful mobility'. We consented that meaningful mobility is not limited to physical prototypes or transportation, but also services or systems that would positively impact the college students in UC Berkeley on their way to campus.

Team One-derland

Our team was composed of students with different majors and nationalities. Our majors were all different including Informatics, Engineering, Economics and more. With this special combination of backgrounds, we came up with intensive and creative ideas to solve the Design Challenge.

Design Process

We learned the Design Process of UC Berkeley in the beginning of the workshop from the Bravo Lab. Our team followed this process and the specifics of each step are on the below inside the workshop poster.


Prototype

Due to the time constraint, we presented our project with the paper prototypes with low fidelity. After the workshop, as a UX Designer, I developed the prototype with Adobe XD and Photoshop for high fidelity.


After the Workshop

After the workshop, our team presented our project at Sungkyunkwan University. With positive feedbacks from the audience, our team won 'The Best Project Award' with great honor.
